Zealer Dashboard

v2.0.0 — Engineering Manager + Engineer cockpit for Jira + Sentry

Roles

Choose your role on first launch (or in Settings → Your role).

  • Engineering Manager — full squad view with team filters. Burndown, team timesheet, and all sprint stories by default. Manage your squad member list from Settings.
  • Engineer — personal view by default. A Me / Squad toggle appears on sprint tickets, time tracking, estimate charts, and support boards. In Me mode the time-tracking charts switch to a personal time-series view: daily bars per sprint day (or monthly bars for quarterly mode) showing your logged hours, with a paired estimate bar when estimates exist on your tickets.

Engineer progress circles

In Engineer mode, two personal donut charts appear at the top of the Today screen (hidden when you have no assigned work):

  • Sprint — your assigned story points broken down by Done (green), In Progress (blue), and Open (slate). Centre shows done / total pts.
  • Support — your assigned support tickets by status. Centre shows done / total. Always based on your assignments regardless of the Me/Squad toggle.

Screens

  • Today — Alert inbox · Insights (Sentry trend, burndown, time logged, estimate vs actual) · Extra boards · Sentry Issues · Current Sprint
  • Sprint Health — Velocity trend, goal hit rate, carry-over, stale tickets
  • Reliability — Sentry 7-day trend, untriaged spikes, SLA adherence, security tickets

Settings

  • Your role — EM or Engineer. Controls which sections and filters are shown.
  • Jira — base URL, email, API token. Squad project key + extra boards (format: Name|BoardID).
  • Sentry — org slug, base URL, auth token. Add as many tracked views as you like.
  • Squad members (EM only) — curate who appears in the team filter. Once saved, auto-discovery stops updating the list.

Milestones (OKRs & Dev Plans)

Add milestones in Settings as label, label|Display Name, or label|Display Name|Leapsome URL — one per line. Tag backlog tickets with the label in Jira and the milestone card tracks them by ticket count (done/total + %), with a status breakdown, an IN SPRINT badge on tickets in the active sprint, and a click-through listing. Engineer Me/Squad scope applies. Add a Leapsome URL to get an "Open in Leapsome" link; otherwise a small hint reminds you to update Leapsome manually.

Demo / Mock Data Mode

Enable via the toggle in Settings (below your role). Fills every chart with realistic mock data — no API calls are made. Great for demos or exploring the app before adding credentials. Turns off automatically on browser restart.

Sprint Timeline (Gantt)

A Gantt chart in the Insights section showing each sprint ticket as a horizontal bar from its start date to its due date. One row per parent story, sorted by priority then Jira rank. The parent's due date is a dashed marker; child tickets (subtasks) appear as bars within the row, one sub-lane per assignee, each positioned by its own start and due date. Childless stories show their own bar. Click any row to open it in Jira. Engineer mode shows only your own tickets. A compact summary row also appears inside the Insights section (above the filters) with a colour-coded progress bar per milestone — click it to scroll to the full card.

Splash screen

A short branded splash (navy background, cap icon, ripple animation, then the "Zealer Dashboard" title) plays once per browser session when you first open the panel. It respects your reduced-motion preference.

Privacy Mode

Click 🔒 in the app bar to toggle screen-share privacy. When ON, personal content is masked while dashboards and metrics remain visible.

Gantt full-tab & PDF

Click the ⤢ icon on the Sprint Timeline header to open the Gantt in a full-width browser tab, then "Save as PDF / Print" (A3 landscape recommended).

Time Logged capacity

In sprint mode, a dashed amber line marks expected hours so far (working days elapsed × 7h). Anyone logging beyond it is flagged ⚠ — a quick "who's over capacity?" read.

Usage analytics

The dashboard reports usage (app opens, feature views, load timing, and handled errors) to the maintainer's Sentry project to help improve the tool, with your Jira identity (email/name) attached so adoption can be tracked. This is completely separate from the Sentry views you configure: your Sentry auth token and issue data go only to your own Sentry org and are never included in this telemetry. There's no third-party tracking and no ads.

My Tasks — personal burndown

In engineer mode (Me scope), the My Tasks card shows a personal sprint burndown beneath your progress donuts: the same ideal/estimate/actual lines as the team burndown, but scoped to the tickets assigned to you and measured in story points. It shares the sprint's window so it lines up with the team chart.

Updates

The dashboard checks on every open for a newer promoted release and shows a banner when one is available, with a link to download it. "Remind me later" hides it for a few days. (Browsers don't let an extension update its own files, so installing the new build is still a manual step for now — a Chrome Web Store listing for true auto-update is planned.)

Time Logged — capacity & pace

The Time Logged chart shows two reference lines at 6h per working day. cap (dashed amber) is the full-sprint budget — total sprint working days × 6h — and stays fixed all sprint; anyone whose logged time passes it is flagged ⚠. pace (dotted grey) is the expected hours so far — elapsed working days × 6h — and moves a little to the right each day, so you can see whether logging is keeping pace.

Bug Reports

In Insights, the Bug Reports card tracks issues of type Bug and QA Bug. Incoming vs Resolved shows, for each of the last 6 sprints, how many bugs were created versus resolved, with a net-flow note so you can see whether the backlog is growing. Open bugs by age shows how many bugs are open now, bucketed by how long they have been open (0–7d / 8–30d / 31–90d / 90d+), plus the median age and a priority breakdown. In Engineer mode with the Me scope, it shows only bugs assigned to you.

Reopen rate shows, over the last 6 sprints, how many bugs were reopened (moved back from Done to an open status) — a quality signal drawn from each bug's history. Open bugs by App groups the open bugs by the App Name field so you can see which app is generating the most.

Monthly Report

Open the monthly report from the 📊 button in the header. The report builds itself from your dashboard data: as you use the dashboard through the month, metrics accumulate, and when the month rolls over the previous month is finalized into history automatically. Each month covers delivery (sprints closed, velocity, completion), bugs (opened, resolved, net flow, open count and median age), support tickets, and hours logged — with a per-engineer breakdown. Use the Squad / Me toggle to switch between the whole squad and just your own footprint.

You can export any month as JSON (the data of record) or a standalone HTML view using the export buttons — this works without any extra permissions. In Settings you can also enable automatic download of each finalized month to your Downloads folder. History is kept for 12 months; one month before the oldest month is removed, the report warns you so you can export it first. Turn on Demo / Mock Data Mode to see the report populated with sample data.
